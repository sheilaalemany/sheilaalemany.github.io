Hi, I'm Sheila!

I am a second-year computer science Ph.D. student at Florida International University (FIU) where I am advised by Dr. Niki Pissinou and supported by the GEM Consortium with MIT Lincoln Lab and FIU Director's Fellowship. Most recently, I was awarded a 2021 NSF Graduate Research Fellowship! My research interests lie in the intersection between cybersecurity and artificial intelligence, also known as adversarial machine learning.

I earned a B.S. in computer science and a B.S in mathematics from FIU in May 2019 [Worlds Ahead Graduate]. Throughout my undergraduate career, my research experience primarily consisted of creating reliable data management techniques for mobile wireless sensor networks. This research resulted in outstanding undergraduate research awards [CRA-E Finalist, FIU SCIS]. Additionally, I was fortunate enough to receive a merit-based scholarship [Great Minds in STEM], and participated in two summer research internships at MIT Lincoln Laboratory.
