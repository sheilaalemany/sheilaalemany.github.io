I am a second-year computer science Ph.D. student at Florida International University (FIU) where I am advised by Dr. Niki Pissinou and supported by the GEM Consortium with MIT Lincoln Lab and FIU Director's Fellowship. My research interests lie in the intersection between cybersecurity and artificial intelligence.

I earned a dual degree with a B.S. in computer science and a B.S in mathematics from FIU in May 2019 [Worlds Ahead Graduate]. Throughout my undergraduate career, my research experience primarily consisted of creating reliable data management techniques for mobile wireless sensor networks. This research resulted in outstanding research awards [CRA-E Finalist, FIU SCIS]. Additionally, I was fortunate enough to receive a merit-based scholarship [Great Minds in STEM], and participate in two summer research internships at MIT Lincoln Laboratory. I also thoroughly enjoyed tutoring mathematics and computer sciences courses.
